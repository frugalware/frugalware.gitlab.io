Xfce 4.8 is on its way

The Xfce development team are hard at work, preparing for the release of 4.8, due in January 2011. They have already made significant improvements, including complete rewrites of the menu, and adding support for remote, shared directories. So that Xfce users can experience and test version 4.8, the team have released Xfce 4.8 pre-release 2. Although it’s marked as a pre-release, it already features much of the extra functionality of 4.8. Thanks to Frugalware developer Devil505, you can easily install this release from a work-in-progress (WIP) repository. Refer to his blog entry for further details.

We are moving

Frugalware started when VMiklos released 0.1 in November 2010. In the early days there were only a few developers and contributors, so we found accommodation in VMiklos' parents' garage. As the team grew though, we had to fit more people into a tiny space. At first it wasn’t too bad because all we had to do was remove the cubicle walls to get more space. Soon even that wasn’t enough and we finally got permission to move the car. Today everyone has a chair and is restricted to a 10-inch netbook, with agreement that everyone keeps their elbows at their side while typing. The growing number of developers and contributors, together with the increasing noise, power costs and the fact that the local donut and coffee shop closed, has meant that we had to move elsewhere. We have bought a building, named it the Frugal-plex (the letter u has a long ooo sound here) and placed a large sculpture in the shape of the Frugalware logo out the front for everyone to see. The new accommodation is great, with everyone having a large cubicle, also free soft drink provided and large lounge areas in which everyone can take a break when they need one. If you are ever in the area, please visit because we’d love to show you around.

Frugalware’s users are people too!

This is the latest in the ongoing series of interviews with Frugalware’s users. Even though we have at least one thing in common - our use of Frugalware - everyone has a different, interesting story to tell.

  1. What’s your name?

    Paolo Cretaro.

  2. What’s your IRC/forum nickname?

    Usually Melko.

  3. In what country do you live?

    I live in Italy (for now).

  4. What operating system(s) did you use before FW ?

    Before 2009 Windows, then I tried several GNU/Linux distros like OpenSuse, Mandriva, Debian, Archlinux, Ubuntu, Pardus, Dreamlinux, Scientific Linux. I actually have a netbook with Frugalware, Sabayon and Slackware.

  5. When did you start to use Linux (Frugalware)?

    I installed my first distro in 2009, before that I only used live CD (like Knoppix) because I was too young to mess up with my parents' PC. Instead I started with Frugalware some months ago, in April-March 2010.

  6. What do you like most about Frugalware?

    First of all the name, I used to define myself a frugal person, so we make a good couple. It’s simple, it’s fast and it has a rolling release tree, what more do you want?

  7. What would you like to see changed in Frugalware?

    Mhm hard question. I like Frugalware as it is now, however I think that it would be great to see more people contributing to this project.

  8. Do you do anything to spread knowledge of Frugalware?

    Not enough. Here Windows rules, so it’s not easy to open peoples' eyes to free software.

  9. For what purpose(s) do you use Frugalware?

    For everything, from gaming to physical computations.

  10. How did you find Frugalware - e.g. a friend/Google search?

    As you can see I tried several distros. One day a friend told me: "Hey, what about trying Frugalware? I’ve never tried it but I’ve heard good things about it.", so I thought "Why not?" and here I am.

  11. What do you do when you’re not using Frugalware?

    I mainly study physics, but also play guitar and take pictures, sometimes I also post them on flickr.

  12. Please show us a screenshot of your desktop(s)

    My desktop has nothing special, I’m not a big fan of appearance.

  13. What’s the view from your front door?

    I haven’t a picture now and I am too lazy to take the camera but I guarantee you that there is nothing special… just other houses…

Colour your man output

most is an output pager which has more sophisticated options than other common pagers such as less and more. One of its features is that it can apply colour to output of the man command. In the default configuration, text which would normally be underlined appears in a light green colour, while switches appear in red. The colours used by most are easily changed. You can see for yourself what the output looks like with the command man most | most. If you want to use most as your default pager, add the following line to your .bashrc file:

PAGER=/usr/bin/most

Focus on Package(s)

autojump

Thanks to Frugalware contributor Fabien, we have a very useful utility added to Frugalware. This is one of those applications which, when I read its description, made me think, "Why hasn’t anyone created this before?" autojump is a command-line utility which allows you to easily cd or jump to a directory you have been to before. The difference between using cd and autojump is that you only need to give autojump part of the destination directory’s name, which saves time and hassle. If you often cd to the directory containing your MP3 files, for example, you might enter j mp3 and you would then be in that directory. If you had visited more than one folder which contained the letters mp3, autojump would take you to the one which you had visited most. It also features an autocompletion feature so you can enter mp3 and press [Tab] and it will either complete the directory’s name if there is just one match, or show you a list of those which match your criteria. It even handles removable media because if the media is not available when you use autojump, any matches on the removable media are ignored.

autojump doesn’t interfere with the cd command’s normal operation so you can choose between the two. If you want to get to somewhere you have been before, you can use autojump, otherwise use cd. If you need to go back there later, autojump can take you there quickly and easily. Try it and see for yourself how much time this little utility saves you.
